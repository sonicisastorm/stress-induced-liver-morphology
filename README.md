# 🧬 Stress-Induced Liver Morphology Research

> Presented at the **XII Republican Competition "Scientists of Tomorrow"**
> Category: Medicine & Health
> Azerbaijan Medical University — Scientific Research Center

---

## 📋 Abstract

This study investigates the morphofunctional changes occurring in liver tissue under conditions of chronic emotional stress. Using a controlled electroshock stress model applied to white laboratory mice over one week, we analyzed both the hematological indicators in blood and the histomorphological changes in liver tissue. Results confirmed that chronic stress causes measurable structural damage to liver tissue, supported by biochemical blood analysis.

---

## 🔬 Methodology

- **Subjects:** 10 white laboratory mice (250–280g), divided into 2 groups of 5
- **Stress Model:** Low-voltage electroshock (45.15V), 30 minutes/day for 1 week, administered in specialized chambers
- **Blood Analysis:** Conducted on Day 8 using Auto Hematology Analyzer RAYTO (RT-7600)
  - Markers measured: ALT, AST, total cholesterol, leukocytes, erythrocytes, thrombocytes
- **Tissue Analysis:** Liver tissue blocks prepared for microscopy and examined under electron microscope at ATU ETM Morphology & Histology Department
- All experiments were conducted in accordance with the **Helsinki Declaration** principles

---

## 📊 Key Findings

### Hematological Changes (vs. control group)
| Indicator | Change |
|-----------|--------|
| Leukocytes | ↓ 12% |
| Erythrocytes | ↑ 11.9% |
| Thrombocytes | ↑ 37.9% |

### Histomorphological Changes
- Dilation of sinusoidal capillaries (especially in zone 3)
- Mild fatty and protein dystrophy in hepatocytes
- Blood stasis and lymphatic vessel enlargement in some capillaries
- Connective tissue element formation (fibrosis) in liver lobules
